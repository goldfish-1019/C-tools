EEE & IPR Exam Cheat Sheet

Business Model Canvas

1. Value Proposition: What exact problem are you solving?

2. Customer Segments: Who are you solving it for?

3. Channels: How does the product reach them?

4. Customer Rel.: How do you interact with them?

5. Revenue Streams: How does the business make money?

6. Key Resources: Assets required (physical/intellectual).

7. Key Activities: Core actions to execute the model.

8. Key Partnerships: Suppliers and allies.

9. Cost Structure: Fixed and variable costs.

Design Thinking Process

1. Empathize: Deeply understand user needs and pain points.

2. Define: State user needs and problems clearly.

3. Ideate: Challenge assumptions, brainstorm creative solutions.

4. Prototype: Start creating simple, tangible solutions.

5. Test: Try your solutions out with actual users.

Types of IPR

Patent: Protects inventions/functionality (Valid for 20 yrs).

Copyright: Protects literary/artistic/software works (Lifetime + 60 yrs).

Trademark: Protects brand identity/logos/slogans (10 yrs, indefinitely renewable).

Trade Secret: Confidential business info (e.g., Coca-Cola recipe).

Geographical Indication (GI): Sign used on products with a specific geographical origin (e.g. Darjeeling Tea).

Patentability Criteria (India)

1. Novelty: Must be entirely new globally, not published anywhere.

2. Non-Obviousness: Must involve an inventive step not obvious to someone skilled in that specific technical art.

3. Industrial Applicability: Must have practical utility and be manufacturable.

Note: Section 3 of Patent Act 1970 lists non-patentable items (e.g., mere discoveries, mathematical methods, software per se).

Startup Funding Stages

1. Bootstrapping: Self-funding by founders without external help.

2. Seed Stage: Angel investors / Friends & Family for early product development and market research.

3. Series A: VC (Venture Capital) funding to scale sales and optimize the product.

4. Series B/C/etc.: Massive scaling, buying out competitors, market expansion.

5. IPO: Initial Public Offering (going public on the stock market).

Pitch Deck Structure

A standard 10-slide Pitch Deck (Guy Kawasaki format):

  • Title: Company name and 1-sentence hook.
  • Problem: The pain point you are solving.
  • Solution: Your product/service.
  • Underlying Magic: Your tech/IP/secret sauce.
  • Business Model: How you make money.
  • Go-to-Market: How you acquire customers.
  • Competitor Analysis: Why you are better.
  • Management Team: Who is building this.
  • Financial Projections: 3-5 year forecast.
  • The Ask: How much funding you need and what for.
